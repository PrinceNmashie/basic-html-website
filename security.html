Security & Social Concerns

Attack surfaces of voice-controlled assistants include security risks like unauthorized data collection and voice imitation, as well as social risks, including dependency on devices that may reduce face-to-face interaction and create solitary environments. Strong encryption, better authentication details, and proper usage tips can only solve these challenges. In connection with the fifth rule – “Make Yourself Look Good Online” the manufacturers have to prove themselves as professional and accountable entities by implementing the latest security features. Software updates, reporting of incidents, and educating users on the correct use of the product are some of the measures that can be taken to address the vulnerabilities. On the social level, the proper usage of devices can be encouraged, and the correct relationship between people and technology can be maintained to avoid overdependence.
